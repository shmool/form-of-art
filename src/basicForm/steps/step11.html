What's next?

There are few thing you can try on your own at this stage:

  • Use css classes (Bootstrap's and/or your own) to design the form and the error messages. You may need to use ngClass. For example, put ng-class="{'alert alert-danger' : demo.myForm.$invalid}" on the ngMessages element.
  • Explore the form and the control attributes.
  • Add more controls and error messages to the form.

In the following session we will stay in the ng neighbourhood, where we will explore:

  • custom validation
  • more options in ngMessages
  • ng-disabled and its problems

The Form of Art series will later continue with advanced issues, including various input types, nested forms, async validation and third-party libraries. Stay tuned!
